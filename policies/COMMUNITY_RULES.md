# GRIDWAKE Community Rules

Version: draft 0.1  
Purpose: keep a tiny cooperative game safe, fair, and truthful.

## The short version

Protect the grid. Respect the people. Do not attack the service. Do not turn a
shared game into a scheme for money, harassment, deception, or disruption.

## Allowed

- unusual, playful, defensive, or experimental strategies within the legal DSL;
- coordinated play and discussion outside GRIDWAKE that does not harass, deceive,
  exclude, or deliberately ruin public rooms;
- good-faith law proposals inside the published safety envelope;
- accessibility tools and supported ChatGPT/Codex controls;
- security research performed under the published disclosure policy and scope.

## Not allowed

- threats, hate, sexual exploitation, harassment, stalking, or encouragement of
  self-harm or violence;
- personal data, doxxing, credentials, malware, unlawful or infringing material;
- impersonation, deceptive callsigns, fake operator messages, or fake receipts;
- flooding, denial of service, capability theft, ban evasion, Sybil room capture,
  or automation represented as human participation;
- exploiting defects, hidden information, timing, connectors, or clients to gain
  unauthorized authority or disrupt other rooms;
- deliberate repeated griefing, including minimally legal policies used to ruin
  public rooms;
- gambling, entry fees, side bets, prizes, tokenization, account sales, or claims
  that GRIDWAKE outcomes have guaranteed economic value;
- attempts to make a world law change identity, moderation, security, privacy,
  authentication, receipts, persistence, governance, or the validator;
- using reports, blocks, or appeals to harass another person or overload review.

## AI-specific rule

Text fields are data, not a way to give instructions to GRIDWAKE's model, server,
moderators, or other players. Attempts to escape the policy/law schema, expose
secrets, override safety instructions, or call unauthorized tools may be rejected
and treated as abuse.

## Enforcement

Depending on context, the operator may warn, reject text, substitute a safe
template, remove a player, restrict public rooms, revoke a share/law, suspend a
session/account, preserve evidence, or contact appropriate services/authorities.
Severe harm can result in immediate action. Enforcement does not change the
canonical result of a completed verified round.

Where appropriate, a notice includes the affected capability, reason category,
duration, policy version, and appeal route. We may withhold details that would
expose another person's data, security controls, or safety evidence.

## Reporting and emergencies

Use in-product `REPORT` or `[SAFETY CONTACT]`. Include the room/law/receipt ID,
category, and only the information needed to understand the issue. Never send a
password or private key.

GRIDWAKE is not an emergency service. If someone is in immediate danger, contact
local emergency services or an appropriate crisis resource.

Security vulnerabilities belong at `[SECURITY POLICY / CONTACT]` so they can be
handled safely and without punishing good-faith research.

## Appeals

Use `[APPEAL URL/CONTACT]` with the notice reference. Appeals are reviewed by a
person not solely responsible for the initial decision where staffing permits.
Submitting repeated duplicate appeals does not accelerate review.
